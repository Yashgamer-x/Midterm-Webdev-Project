About

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist whose work reshaped our understanding of space, time, and energy. Best known for developing the theory of relativity and the iconic equation E = mc2, he also made groundbreaking contributions to quantum mechanics and statistical physics.

Born in 1879 in Ulm, Germany, Einstein's curiosity and imagination led him to challenge conventional scientific thought. He received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, a key step in the development of quantum theory.

Beyond science, Einstein was a passionate violinist, a deep thinker, and an advocate for peace and civil rights. His legacy continues to inspire generations of scientists, philosophers, and dreamers.
